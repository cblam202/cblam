About

I am an economist focused on labor markets, education policy, and quantitative methods. My work integrates data-driven insights with real-world applications to inform better policy design.

Teaching

I teach undergraduate and graduate courses in econometrics, labor economics, and applied statistics. I emphasize hands-on learning using real-world datasets and modern statistical tools.

Research

My research explores how education and training shape labor outcomes, with a focus on rural-urban inequality. Current projects include the use of machine learning in educational policy analysis and causal inference methods in workforce development.
